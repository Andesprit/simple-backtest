# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is Python developers and quantitative hobbyists who want to test long-only trading ideas against their own OHLC(V) data. They need to understand the accounting model, inspect the strategy logic, and compare results without adopting a large trading platform.

## Product Purpose

Simple Backtest makes focused strategy testing straightforward and inspectable. The public site should help a technically literate visitor understand the library quickly, trust its deliberately limited scope, and either install it or inspect the source and examples.

## Positioning

A small, transparent, bring-your-own-data backtesting framework for long-only strategies—not an opaque trading platform.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: install Simple Backtest from PyPI.
- Secondary CTA: explore the source and examples on GitHub.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: bring your data and strategy; Simple Backtest handles the backtest.
- Belief ladder: the framework is easy to start, honest about its accounting scope, flexible enough for custom strategies, and capable of useful comparison, optimization, and visualization.
- Proof on hand: concise working Python examples, explicit supported-asset boundaries, 20+ documented metrics, built-in optimization tools, Plotly visualizations, and six runnable notebooks.

## Brand Personality

Precise, quietly confident, and technically honest. The experience should create calm confidence through clear explanations and real product artifacts rather than trading hype.

## Anti-references

Avoid speculative-performance claims, trading-floor theatrics, generic dark terminal styling, stock SaaS card grids, and visuals that imply unsupported shorting, leverage, derivatives, or live execution.

## Design Principles

1. Show the product working through real code and believable outputs.
2. Make scope a trust signal rather than hiding limitations.
3. Let visitors move from understanding to installation without friction.
4. Prefer technical clarity over marketing superlatives.
5. Keep the marketing surface independent from the Python package.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and interaction requirements. Support keyboard navigation, clear focus states, readable code at narrow widths, touch-friendly targets, and a reduced-motion experience.
